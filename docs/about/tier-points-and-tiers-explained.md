# Tier Points and Tiers Explained

This guide explains how Elite Training turns your results into **tier points** and a **tier label** in a simple way.

## The short version

- You get one percentage for each core skill:
  - Pot success
  - Position outcome
  - Rack conversion
- These combine into **tier points** (a continuous progress number).
- Your **tier label** (Beginner, Amateur, Strong Amateur, Advanced, Semi-pro, Elite) is not based on points alone.
- Labels are protected by **minimum skill floors** from your Settings, so a weak area can hold your label back even if points are high.

That is intentional: points show momentum, labels show overall readiness.

## What tier points mean

Tier points are your progress meter.

- They move up and down smoothly as your percentages change.
- They are weighted (Position, Conversion, Potting can have different importance).
- They include a balance check (imbalance penalty) so one very strong KPI does not hide weak KPIs.

Think of points as: **“How well are you performing overall right now?”**

## What the tier label means

Tier labels represent a minimum standard across all three skills.

Each tier in Settings has KPI minimums.  
To be labeled in a tier, you must meet all minimums for that tier.

Example:
- Your points may land in an Advanced points band.
- But if Position or Conversion is below Advanced minimums, the label is capped lower.

Think of label as: **“What level are you consistently ready for across all skills?”**

## Why points and label can differ

This is normal and useful.

- **Points higher than label** means: progress is strong, but one skill still needs to catch up.
- **Points and label aligned** means: your performance and minimum requirements are both on target.

## How to use this in practice

1. Use **tier points** to track short-term momentum.
2. Use the **tier label** to track true all-around level.
3. If points are climbing but label is stuck, check which KPI is below the next tier minimum.
4. Focus training on that limiting KPI to unlock the next label.

## In the Settings sandbox

The sandbox follows the same live logic:

- points are calculated continuously
- label is gated by tier minimums

If points and label differ, the sandbox explains why.
